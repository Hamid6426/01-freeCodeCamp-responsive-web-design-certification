Dr. Israr Ahmed

the Legacy of a Remarkable Scholar

Here's a time line of Dr. Israr Ahmed life:

  • 1932 - Born in Haryana, India
  • 1950 - After graduating from high school, attended the King Edward Medical University.
  • 1954 - Received his MBBS degree from King Edward Medical University in 1954 and began practising medicine.
  • 1965 - Obtained his master's degree in Islamic Studies from the University of Karachi.
  • 1971 - Abandoned his thriving medical practice to dedicate himself fully to the Islamic revival.
  • 1972 - Established the Markazi Anjuman Khuddam-ul-Qur’an Lahore.
  • 1975 - Founded Tanzeem-e-Islami.
  • 1981 - Awarded the Sitara-i-Imtiaz, Pakistan’s third-highest civilian honor.
  • 1991 - Launched Tahreek-e-Khilafat Pakistan.
  • 2010 - dies at the age of 77.
  • 2017 - Wrote around 60 books on Islam and Pakistan, of which twenty nine have been translated into several other languages, including in English.
